Elizabeth S Friedman was a female American expert in cryptanalyst, which deciphered enemy codes in both World Wars and helped solve international smuggling cases during Prohibition.

Elizabeth S Friedman and her husband worked for the U.S. government in the 1920s. They wrote the book on code-breaking for the U.S. Army and had the first cryptography class for soldiers during WWI.

During WWII, she worked as the Coast Giard in the Navy for detecting and monitoring clandestine spy transmissions. Her group solved numerous cipher systems that were used by Germans and their local sympathizers; also decoded 4,000 messages sent on 48 different radio circuits.
